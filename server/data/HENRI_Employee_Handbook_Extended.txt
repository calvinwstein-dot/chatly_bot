
HENRI – EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK & HR POLICIES
Effective Date: January 2026
Internal Use Only

--------------------------------------------------
1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------
HENRI is a fictional Denmark-based technology and professional services company with approximately 75 employees.
The company operates with a hybrid workforce and emphasizes transparency, autonomy, and accountability.

HENRI’s mission is to deliver high-quality digital solutions while maintaining a healthy, respectful,
and inclusive workplace for all employees.

--------------------------------------------------
2. EMPLOYMENT TYPES & CONTRACTS
--------------------------------------------------
Employees may be engaged as full-time, part-time, or fixed-term contractors.
All employment relationships are governed by Danish employment law.

All new employees are subject to a three-month probation period. During this time,
either party may terminate employment with shorter notice.

--------------------------------------------------
3. WORKING HOURS & ATTENDANCE
--------------------------------------------------
Standard working hours are 37 hours per week, Monday to Friday.
Core working hours are from 09:00 to 15:00.

Flexible scheduling may be agreed upon with a manager based on role requirements.

--------------------------------------------------
4. COMPENSATION & REMUNERATION
--------------------------------------------------
Salaries are paid monthly in arrears and deposited directly into the employee’s
designated Danish bank account.

Salary reviews are conducted annually based on performance and company results.

Overtime may be compensated financially or through time off in lieu,
depending on role and agreement.

--------------------------------------------------
5. LEAVE & ABSENCE POLICIES
--------------------------------------------------
Employees are entitled to 25 days of paid annual leave per year in accordance
with Danish holiday legislation.

Danish Public Holidays:
- New Year's Day (1 January)
- Maundy Thursday (varies, Thursday before Easter)
- Good Friday (varies, Friday before Easter)
- Easter Monday (varies, Monday after Easter)
- Great Prayer Day (varies, 4th Friday after Easter)
- Ascension Day (varies, 40 days after Easter)
- Whit Monday (varies, 50 days after Easter)
- Constitution Day (5 June, half day)
- Christmas Day (25 December)
- Second Day of Christmas (26 December)

Note: Most Danish holidays are movable and depend on Easter dates. New Year's Day, Constitution Day, and Christmas are fixed dates.

Sick Leave:
Employees must notify their manager before the start of the workday.
Medical documentation may be required for extended absences.

--------------------------------------------------
6. ONBOARDING & OFFBOARDING
--------------------------------------------------
New employees participate in a structured onboarding program that includes:
- System access setup
- Review of company policies
- Role-specific training
- Introduction to team members

Upon termination of employment, employees must:
- Return all company equipment
- Transfer knowledge where applicable
- Complete an exit process coordinated by HR

--------------------------------------------------
7. STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (SOPs)
--------------------------------------------------
SOPs define how recurring tasks and processes are performed at HENRI.
Employees are expected to follow documented SOPs.

Key SOP areas include:
- Requesting annual leave
- Reporting sickness or absence
- Submitting expense claims
- Onboarding new team members
- Escalating HR or workplace concerns
- Handling confidential information
- IT access requests and offboarding

--------------------------------------------------
8. CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------------------------------------
All employees must behave professionally and respectfully.

Harassment, discrimination, bullying, or inappropriate behavior of any kind
will not be tolerated.

Employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues with respect
- Act ethically and responsibly
- Follow company policies and procedures

--------------------------------------------------
9. REMOTE & HYBRID WORK POLICY
--------------------------------------------------
Employees may work remotely up to three days per week,
subject to manager approval and business needs.

Employees working remotely are expected to:
- Remain reachable during core hours
- Maintain productivity standards
- Protect company data and systems

--------------------------------------------------
10. IT, DATA PROTECTION & SECURITY
--------------------------------------------------
Company IT systems are provided for business use only.

Employees must:
- Protect login credentials
- Comply with GDPR requirements
- Report any suspected data breaches immediately
- Avoid using unauthorized software or devices

--------------------------------------------------
11. HEALTH, SAFETY & WELLBEING
--------------------------------------------------
HENRI is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment.

Employees are encouraged to:
- Raise health or safety concerns
- Support colleagues’ wellbeing
- Maintain a healthy work-life balance

--------------------------------------------------
12. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT & DEVELOPMENT
--------------------------------------------------
Employees participate in annual performance reviews.

Managers provide ongoing feedback and support employee development
through training and growth opportunities.

--------------------------------------------------
13. HR SUPPORT & ESCALATION
--------------------------------------------------
Employees should first consult the internal HR Assistant
for HR-related questions and guidance.

If further support is required, employees may contact HR directly at:
hr@henri.internal

--------------------------------------------------
END OF DOCUMENT
